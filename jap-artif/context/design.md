# Japanese Artifacts Website — Design Context (Section-wise)

> **This is the design context for Claude Code/Codex. It describes how every section should look, feel, animate, and interact.**

---

# Overall Design Philosophy

The website should feel like walking inside a quiet Japanese museum.

Not a shopping website.

Not a portfolio.

Not a template.

Every section should breathe.

Everything should have generous whitespace.

Nothing should feel rushed.

The visual identity should combine

* Wabi Sabi
* Minimalism
* Luxury Editorial
* Japanese Museum
* Traditional Craftsmanship
* Apple-level polish

---

# 01 — Hero Section

## Purpose

Immediately establish premium craftsmanship and emotional connection.

The visitor should instantly understand

> "This website is about timeless Japanese craftsmanship."

---

## Layout

```
------------------------------------------

Navigation

------------------------------------------

Large Japanese Heading

Description

CTA

                     Hero Artifact

                     Rising Sun

                     Ink Brush

                     Cherry Blossom

------------------------------------------
```

The hero artifact occupies around **45% of the screen**.

Text occupies around **35%**.

Remaining space stays empty.

Whitespace is part of the design.

---

## Background

Off-white handmade paper texture.

Large faded red sun.

Very soft ink landscape.

Tiny floating dust particles.

Brush textures.

Cherry blossom branch entering from top-right.

No busy graphics.

---

## Artifact

Centerpiece

Example

* Tea Bowl
* Kintsugi Bowl
* Ceramic Vase

Placed on a black stone platform.

Large soft shadow.

Museum lighting.

---

## Animation

Page loads

Paper fades in.

Sun scales from 0.9 to 1.

Artifact fades while slowly rotating.

Brush stroke draws itself.

Japanese heading reveals using mask animation.

CTA slides upward.

Particles begin floating.

Mouse movement

Artifact tilts

2°

Entire hero slowly breathes forever.

---

## CTA

Black button.

Gold border on hover.

Arrow slides.

Scale 1.03.

# Final Feeling

When someone lands on the website, it should feel like:

* Entering a quiet Kyoto craft museum.
* Every artifact has a story.
* Every animation feels handcrafted.
* Every section transitions like turning the page of a premium art book.
* The entire experience is calm, immersive, and luxurious, with Japanese craftsmanship taking center stage rather than flashy effects.
